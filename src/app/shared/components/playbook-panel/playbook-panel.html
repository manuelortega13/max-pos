Pricing & operations for a student-focused store

price_change Markup by category Blended target: 90–120%

Don't apply one fixed % to everything. Each category has a natural ceiling. The blended average emerges from the mix.

CategoryMarkupWhy
Cooked food
rice meals, fried chicken, siopao, siomai		 150–200% Your moneymaker. No competition within steps of the gate.
Hotdog bun 100–150% Fast mover, easy to prep, high margin.
Counter impulse
candies, gum, mints, ₱5–10 treats		 80–120% Students aren't price-shopping at the till.
Snacks
chichirya, biscuits, viral TikTok items		 60–100% Trend-driven; price flexibility on most items.
Soft drinks 25–40% Competitive pressure caps this. Don't fight it — they're your traffic builder.

How blended works: if cooked food is 40%+ of your revenue, you'll naturally land at 90–120% blended without setting any single price differently. Lift the cooked-food share of revenue = lift the blended markup, no price tweaks needed.

rule Pricing rules
  • Use round denominations — ₱5, ₱10, ₱15, ₱20, ₱25, ₱30, ₱40, ₱50. Never ₱13, ₱17, ₱27, ₱33. Students count change.
  • Bundle meal + drink at a small discount — e.g. rice + chicken + Coke = ₱80 (vs ₱85 separately). Turns a ₱45 sale into an ₱80 sale.
  • Hold soft-drink prices steady. Don't price-fight your competitor on Coke. The proximity-to-gate advantage is worth more than ₱2.
  • Counter impulse shelf at eye level — mentos, frutella, ₱5–10 candies. Lifts daily revenue 10–20% with near-zero cost.
  • Combo signs above the chiller — make bundles visible without having to ask.

Recess is your Christmas — every single day. Pre-prep saves you from losing sales when students arrive and the kitchen is cold.

Cardinal sin: students arriving at recess and you're still frying chicken. They'll go to the competitor. Don't give them the chance.

savings Cash flow & summer survival April–May is your biggest risk
  • Save ₱500/day in a separate "summer fund" envelope. By April you'll have ~₱45–60k buffer.
  • Target: 6–8 weeks of operating expenses in cash before April–May hits.
  • Track spoilage daily. Notebook on the counter — what got thrown out at close? Sum it monthly. Hidden cost recovery: ~₱30–50k/year.
  • Real fixed cost is probably ₱40–45k/month once LPG, packaging, spoilage, and shrinkage are included. The ₱33k figure under-counts.
  • April–May pivot: switch the menu to neighborhood items (halo-halo, ice candy, sago't gulaman). 100–200% margin, doesn't depend on students.
  • Cut hours in summer — open 9am–4pm instead of 6am–7pm. Save labor + utilities.
 trending_up Growth tactics
  • Chase TikTok-viral snacks weekly. Be the first near the school to stock the new viral item. 2–3 weeks of premium pricing before the supermarket catches up.
  • Loyalty card for college students. 5 meals = 1 free softdrink. Trivial cost, strong retention.
  • Group/dorm orders for college students. "3 chicken meals: ₱220" (vs ₱270 separately). Group buys are pure gold.
  • Know their names. Students love being recognized. Costs ₱0, raises retention dramatically.
  • Better wholesale prices > higher retail prices. A 5% better cost is invisible to customers but equivalent to a 7–10% markup increase.
 block Skip these
  • Cigarettes — legally risky with minors, PR-toxic, low margin. Drop them.
  • Big-bag snacks (₱80+) — students don't have the budget. Stock ₱15–25 sizes 2–3× deep instead.
  • Adult-oriented slow movers — canned goods, condiments. They take shelf space from items that actually turn.
  • Don't expand SKUs to match competitor. Stay focused on what students actually buy.
 checklist This week's checklist 4 actions that move the needle
  1. Measure true cost-per-meal for your top 5 cooked items. Spreadsheet, 30 minutes. Include LPG share, packaging, and spoilage buffer.
  2. Start a spoilage log. Notebook on the counter. Write down what's thrown out at close. Sum it at month-end.
  3. Print a menu board with bundle prices. Visible to anyone in line. No more "ano meron?" 30 times a day.
  4. Open the summer fund envelope. Put ₱500/day in starting now. By April you'll have ₱45–60k buffer.
